INTRODUCTION
------------
Welcome to the Command Handling AI Operating System (C.H.A.O.S.). This is not 
just a voice assistant; it is a comprehensive AI companion designed to bridge 
the gap between you and your Windows operating system. Unlike standard assistants 
that simply perform basic tasks, CHAOS is built with a complex personality engine, 
local AI intelligence, and deep system integration.

This manual documents every feature of CHAOS, explaining how to use them and 
how they function to enhance your daily workflow.

1. CORE INTERACTION & MODES
================================================================================

CHAOS is designed to be flexible, allowing you to interact with it in the way 
that best suits your current environment.

VOICE VS. TYPE INPUT
--------------------
By default, CHAOS operates in **Voice Mode**, listening for your commands through 
your microphone. This provides a hands-free experience similar to JARVIS or Friday. 
However, if you are in a noisy environment or a quiet library, you can switch to 
**Type Mode**. In this mode, CHAOS stops listening and instead waits for you to 
type commands via the terminal.

*   **Command:** "Type mode" - Disables the microphone and prompts for text input.
*   **Command:** "Voice mode" - Re-enables the microphone for voice commands.

OUTPUT CONTROL
--------------
You can also control how CHAOS responds. In **Voice Output Mode**, the AI will 
speak its responses using a high-quality text-to-speech engine. If you prefer 
silence, you can switch to **Text Only Mode**, where CHAOS will simply print its 
responses to the console window.

*   **Command:** "Turn on voice" / "Switch to voice"
*   **Command:** "Text mode" / "Switch to text"

THE WAKE WORD SYSTEM
--------------------
To prevent CHAOS from listening when you don't want it to, it features a Wake Word 
system. When enabled, CHAOS enters a low-power listening state and will only 
respond after it hears "Hey CHAOS" or "CHAOS Activate".

*   **Command:** "Enable wake word" - Starts waiting for the trigger phrase.
*   **Command:** "Disable wake word" - Listens for all commands continuously.

THE INTERACTIVE MENU
--------------------
For users who prefer a graphical interface approach without leaving the terminal, 
CHAOS includes a comprehensive **Interactive Settings Menu**. This menu allows 
you to toggle all the above settings, change personalities, and view features 
using simple number keys (1-9), eliminating the need to remember specific commands 
for configuring the system.

*   **Command:** "Menu" or "Open settings" - Launches the interactive menu.

3. AI BRAIN & INTELLIGENCE (OLLAMA INTEGRATION)
================================================================================

CHAOS uses **Ollama**, a local Large Language Model runner, as its "brain." This 
allows it to answer complex questions, write code, summarizes text, and reason 
about problems locally on your machine, without sending data to the cloud.

INTELLIGENT CONVERSATION
------------------------
When the AI Brain is enabled, you can ask CHAOS anything. It handles general 
knowledge queries, coding questions, and creative writing tasks.
*   **Usage:** Just ask naturally, e.g., "Why is the sky blue?" or "Write a python script to sort a list."

CONTEXT AWARENESS
-----------------
CHAOS possesses a short-term conversational memory. It remembers the last few 
exchanges, allowing for natural follow-up questions.
*   **Example:** 
    User: "Who directed Inception?"
    CHAOS: "Christopher Nolan directed Inception."
    User: "What else did **he** make?" (CHAOS understands "he" refers to Nolan).

MANUAL AI TOGGLE
----------------
You have full control over when the heavy AI model is used. You can toggle the 
AI Brain ON or OFF via the **Menu** (Option 4).
*   **AI OFF:** CHAOS runs in "System Mode," responding instantly to commands like "Open Chrome" but replying "I don't know" to complex questions. This is faster and uses less RAM.
*   **AI ON:** CHAOS routes unknown complex queries to the local LLM for intelligent answers.

EXPLICIT OVERRIDE
-----------------
Even if the AI Brain is disabled, you can force a smart response by using specific trigger phrases:
*   **Command:** "Ask Ollama [Question]"
*   **Command:** "Ask AI [Question]"
